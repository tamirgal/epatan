Pauline,.. born in Rochester, New York.
Pauline is a student at the Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, Maryland.  She is majoring in Art History with an emphasis on the Italian Renaissance.  After graduating from Hopkins, she plans to go on to medical school.
As her parents, we have had the keenest pleasure raising her.  It is now the turn of those whose life she touches to enjoy her multiple talents and her generous heart!  She is unmatched in the breadth of her interests, which she executes with verve, polish and precision.  The day she dissected a frozen squirrel while we gazed incredulous and fascinated will remain forever etched in our mind for the precision, the authority and the stunning quality of her performance! 
We named Pauline after her wonderful grandmother Pauline Cohen Israel whose kindness, intelligence and indomitable spirit are legendary.  Pauline resembles her grandmother not only physically, but also in spirit, in mind and in the strength of her character.  When Pauline was a toddler and we were together in a pool in the scorching sun, I, her mother, remarked that the sun was "very very strong" that day.  Pauline, whom I was holding in my arms, stared at me with a look of incomprehension, offended by the remark, she asked anxiously "stronger than me?"   "No" I said "nobody and nothing is stronger than you"!  Pauline carries that confidence in a quiet and unassuming way.  I hope you get a chance to know her one day!

Chloe,.. born in Rochester, New York.
Chloe loves soccer, pizza and playing silly with her friends!  We call her the ice storm baby because the week she was born a great big ice storm hit Rochester, New York.  The city practically shut down due to falling tree branches on power lines. Without heat or electricity, we had to spend the week before Chlo?'s birth at our good friends' Ruth Gross and Hans Kellner.  The experience was exciting and the city was dazzling all covered in ice.  Thus was born a most delightful child!
Her speed, her imagination and her inexhaustible energy coupled with her unswerving sense of justice and her love of beauty have made her the apple of our eyes.  May you all have a chance to get to know her and listen to her play the violin or the piano.  She plays with heart, with intelligence and with a speed that has made even her better teachers respectful!
Chloe's middle name, Madeleine, derives from that of her paternal grandmother, Madeleine Boucher Pelletier, a woman of so much kindness and profound inner beauty.  We chose her first name, Chlo?, because, pronounced in English -- as it would often be in the United States -- it sounded like a resounding and joyful French" oui," or YES!  and pronounced in French it would ring out a gleeful and boisterous "oh?"!
